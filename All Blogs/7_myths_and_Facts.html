7 Myths and Facts about Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is an important topic in today’s digital age. With increasingly sensitive information being stored online, we must understand the myths and facts surrounding cyber security to increase awareness and reduce threats. Unfortunately, no one can predict or assure if a device, network, or server is secure for life. Technologies are prone to hacking, no matter how advanced they are. Cyberattacks happen to steal, hack, or violate confidential data and information that can be sold for money or used for blackmail purposes. Let’s look at some of the common myths and facts about cybersecurity:


Myth 1: Antivirus software is sufficient to stay safe online.

Fact: Although antivirus software is an essential component of cybersecurity, it’s not enough to protect yourself online. The software can protect your device from certain malware and viruses, but it can’t prevent all cyber threats. To stay safe online, you must use strong passwords, update your software regularly and use two-factor authentication.


Myth 2: Cyber attackers don’t target small and medium-sized companies

Fact: Cyberattacks can happen to anyone, irrespective of the size of your business. In fact, attackers often target small businesses and individuals as they don’t have the same level of cybersecurity as larger companies. That’s why cyber awareness is a must in every organisation, big or small.In addition, cybercriminals often use various methods to target small businesses and individuals with phishing scams and social engineering.

Myth 3: Strong passwords are enough to prevent a data breach

Fact: Many companies believe that strong internal passwords with standard procedures are sufficient for a secure workflow. However, while a robust password application is the first step, you also need multi-layered security to truly protect your data and your company’s vital information. This includes data masking, 2FA, database access monitoring, and more.

Myth 4: Cyber Threats Come from the Outside

Fact: External cyberattacks can indeed harm your company, but sometimes, internal threats can be even more fatal. They can arise from an attempt made by an employee to seek revenge or financial gain against the company or through a mistake from someone who hasn’t received cybersecurity training. An IBM report suggests that internal threats cost companies over $4.61 million annually. Therefore, it’s essential to give cybersecurity information to everyone in your organisation and be vary of internal threats.

Myth 5: Cyberattacks only happen to those who are irresponsible or careless online

Fact: While some cyberattacks can result from user error, many others result from sophisticated tactics used by cybercriminals. Even the most tech-savvy individuals can fall prey to phishing emails. That’s why you must stay vigilant and practice the best cybersecurity habits. Even those who are extremely careful can become victims of cyberattacks.


Myth 6: Cybersecurity is time-consuming and expensive.

Fact: Yes, it requires time and money, but the consequences of not focusing on computer security can be way more expensive. Any data breach or cyberattack can result in legal fees, lost revenue, loss of sensitive data, and damage to your brand’s reputation. When you weigh the costs of implementing cybersecurity measures against the potential cost of a cyberattack, you’ll realise it’s a valuable investment for your business.


Myth 7: We will learn immediately if our company information is compromised

Fact: Cyberattacks can be hidden for months or years till someone finds out about the breach. Malware and safety menaces can be super challenging to detect, even for cyber security companies. Since cybercriminals and their technologies continue to evolve, it’s essential to keep an eye out for anything suspicious. Cyber security threats aren’t always screamed out loud, so you may need to be extra vigilant at work.


For your business to stay safe online, it’s important to understand cyber security information that can protect your people and assets. While antivirus software is essential to cybersecurity, it’s not enough to keep you safe online. Cyberattacks can happen to anyone, irrespective of the size or level of cyber security awareness in your organisation.
